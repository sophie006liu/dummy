How I Came to Like Computational Biology

My enthusiasm for biology was jump started by a tiny amoeba I saw under a microscope in 6th grade and my amazing biology teacher in 8th grade, which led to me to join a high school International Genetically Engineered Machine (iGEM) team.

At the 2018 Pacific Northwest iGEM meetup, I barely understood a presentation on In-Situ protein interaction sequencing, but the pictures looked so cool that I immediately researched the lab at home, and I discovered the wonderful world of de novo protein design, protein folding, and more!

As I researched about protein science, I quickly realized that the problems we face with proteins are biological and computational problems!
